PvZ Heroes: Reimagined

Extras

  1. Differences between Plants and Zombies:
    2.
    • Plants can attack the turn they're played, zombies cannot, and need Haste to do so.
    • Plants generally have lower Health than Zombies.
    • Team-up is a lot more common in Plants
    • Double Strike is exclusive to Plants
    • Frenzy is exclusive to Zombies.
    • When a Plant is destroyed, it goes to the discard pile. When a Zombie is destroyed, it goes to the graveyard.
    • Recruit is exclusive to Plants
    • Revive is exclusive to Zombies.
  2. Traits and Effects:
    3.
      2.1 Traits:
    • Haste: This Zombie can attack on the turn it is played.
    • Team-up: You can fit 2 fighters her.e
    • Short-sighted: This can not attack the opponent's Dummies.
    • Double Strike: When this hurt a Zombie, this does a bonus attack.
    • Frenzy: When this destroy a Plant, this does a bonus attack.
    • Untrickable: Unaffected by opponent's Tricks.
    • Strikethrough: This hits all opponent's Fighters and Dummies in this lane.
    • Splash Damage: When this attack, this also deals the listed damage to Fighters next doors.
    • Amphibious: This card can be played on Water or Land.
    • Armored: Reduces damage taken by this card by the listed amount.
    • Shielded: Negates the first instance of damage taken by this card.
    • Prepare: This card require waiting an extra turn before attacking.
    • Bullseye: This card ignore all opposing fighters while attacking and attack the opposing Dummy.
    • Evolution: Play this on the listed Class to use this ability.
    • Fusion: Play a Fighter on this to use this ability.
    • Hunt: When a Fighter is played, this Fighter move to the same lane as them.
    • Hybrid: A combination of Suns and Brains can be used to play this card.
      2.2 Effects:
    • Recruit: Reveal cards from your deck until you found the listed amount of listed types of cards. Put the rest of the revealed cards on the bottom of the deck in the same order they were picked up.
    • Revive: Add the listed amounts of listed types of cards from your graveyard and play them.
    • Chill: Divide the Fighter's Strength by half, rounded down, last until after its next attack.
    • Freeze: Reduce a Fighter's Strength to 0, last until after its next attack.
    • Stun: Reduce a Fighter's Strength to 0 and prevent it from using any abilities, last until after its next attack.
    • Bounce: Put a Fighter back into its owner's hand.
    • Homing: During combat, choose any fighters to attack.
  3. Classes:
    4.
      2.1 Plants:
    • Appease-mint: Peas, single-shooting and straight hitting Plants.
    • Enlighten-mint: Sun-producing and Sun-related Plants.
    • Reinforce-mint: Walls, defensive and supporting Plants.
    • Pepper-mint: Explosive and fire Plants.
    • Contain-mint: Freeze, Freeze-related and stalling Plants.
    • Enforce-mint: Melee and non-attack damaging Plants.
    • Ail-mint: Mushrooms.
    • Spear-mint: Strikethrough, Strikethrough-related and electric Plants.
    • Enchant-mint: Magic and non-lawn support Plants.
    • Arma-mint: Pult, lobbing and Heights-related Plants.
      2.2 Zombies:
    • Hearty: High-health and armored Zombies.
    • Swarmy: Comes in groups or spawn groups of Zombies.
    • Feisty: Haste or quick damage Zombies.
    • Helpy: Brain producing, Brain-related and supporting Zombies.
    • Crazy: Explosive and Frenzy Zombies.
    • Sneaky: Non-damaging, Untrickable and Amphibious Zombies.
    • Techy: Machine and Technology related Zombies.
    • Speciality: Magic-related, element-related and specific counter Zombies.
  4. Gamemodes:
    5.
    • Standard: Standard gameplay described in the rules.
    • Simplified: Players play on one lawn, each choose one side to play as.
    • Extended: Similar to that of Standard, but once a player lose 3 dummies on any side. The game is not over. Instead destroy everything on that lawn, and nothing can be played there from now on. Then, the losing players gets 2 extra resources and draws 1 extra card of the side that did not lose. Both player no longer draw cards from the deck of the side thats no longer played. The losing players have 3 turns to beat the winning players on the last lawn to win, otherwise they lose.
    • Classic Vs: Instead of using a deck, each player picks sides and chooses 1 card of their side back and forth until both players have chosen 6 cards. During the Start Phase, the turn player adds cards from outside the game so that they have one of each card chosen at the start of the game. A player can only pick 1 Legendary card.
